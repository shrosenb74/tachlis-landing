Question

The shofar is blown on Rosh Hashanah. What is it actually made from?

A A carved wooden horn
B A ram's horn
C A type of brass instrument
D A clay pipe
Correct. A shofar is made from a ram's horn — no mouthpiece, no keys, just breath and lip pressure. It's one of the oldest instruments in the world, and it sounds exactly like you'd expect a parking-lot trumpet from 3,000 BC to sound. That's the point. It's supposed to be raw.
